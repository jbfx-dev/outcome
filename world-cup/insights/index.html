Badges can drive user actions, when paired with strong distribution and strong product offer however 84% of badge depositors were one-and-done. However, that's not to say they can't be reactivated again.

Redesign future badge mechanics to require trading activity, not just deposits. Automate deposit bonuses if used, to give the user immediate spending power while the impulse exists. The Three Things That Must Be True
1 Outcome can measure what it markets.
The attribution gap must be closed. Without it, every dollar allocation is a guess. This is the load-bearing wall. If it doesn't hold, nothing else in this plan works.
2 The product retains users beyond the incentive.
The Jun 7 cohort's 30.2% Week 5 retention proves it's possible. The Jul 5 cohort's 15.9% Week 1 proves incentive design can destroy it. The next campaign's incentive structure must select for traders, not depositors.
3 Outcome can generate volume without a World Cup.
$29.4M during the biggest sporting event in the world is impressive. $40K/day the moment it ends is sobering. If Outcome can establish a $50K+/day non-event floor, this is a protocol. If it can't, this is a promotional business that activates every 4 years.
